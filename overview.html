Overview

For Organizations and Researchers

We understand that research managers need reliable data to make strategic decisions. What’s more, data must be open, free, transparent, accurate, well organized and up-to-date.

Scientilla team thinks that the only way to achieve this goal is to offer a free, powerful, flexible and easy tool for the scientific community.

Scientilla allows you to navigate the world of research and define your research. Your scientific production is important and it will be more useful if you share it. With your collaboration and our know-how data will be always open, complete and clean.

The world of research has several challenges:

Scientilla team answers the most pressing challenges research institutions face with an innovative tool that is able to solve problems. In an ever-changing R&D landscape, today’s research managers need to ensure expected outcomes and take the right decisions to measure the quality and impact of research projects. To accomplish these objectives, research leaders must be able to:

  • Define correct and complete data.
  • Work with other institutions and help researchers collaborate.
  • Assess both individual researcher’s and team performance for recruitment and retention decisions.
  • Gather comprehensive and reliable data to build research strategies, as well as analyze and report on results, for example to support national assessment exercises.
  • Identify the institution’s strengths and weaknesses and determine which areas of research to invest in.
  • Locate funding opportunities and track outcomes.
  • Promote the organization’s achievements.
  • Make benchmark research performance against peer.

Users benefits:

Scientilla provide users with a wide view, from panoramic to granular details.

  • Organization committee (strategic level): to define strategic planning; track research results; understand the organization’s research strengths; benchmark and optimize funding application and success rates.
  • Research administrators (organization level): to create management-level reports; promote institutional and cross-institutional collaboration; obtain and support large grants; find the right opportunities and build the right teams and show researchers’ expertise.
  • Leaders of working group (groups of any size level): to retain and recruit researchers; gain objective insights of team performance; benchmark; win large grants.
  • Researchers (single unit level): to increase their visibility and highlight achievements; expand networking; locate collaborators and mentors and find the right funding opportunities.

We answer the following questions:

  • “Collecting data on my working group takes me too long and is hard to do. How can I make this more efficient and be sure that gained data is reliable and complete?”
  • “How can I effectively track our research activities to share useful information all around the world?”
  • “Why should I show only a part of my scientific productivity and consequently be assessed on this partial data?
  • “I desire to get a more granular breakdown of an organization level. How can I identify the group of work activities?”
  • “I need to detect researchers operating around the world to encourage collaboration and corporate partnerships. How can I achieve best results with a minimum effort?”
  • “I want to benchmark my organizations against its peers, and my working group also want to benchmark themselves. How can we do this effectively to suit our different realities?”
  • “I need to know how large is a peer group in order to normalize the outputs with the actual number of involved researchers. How to know the size of a working group around the world? ”
  • “Which is the best way to show our unique strengths in order to secure funding and attract talents?”

Scientilla Identifies the Scientific Profiles of Researchers and Organizations proposing an innovative software solution. Scientilla allows you and your colleagues to validate, organize and share, with the scholarly community, the bibliographic metadata of your scientific production as well as to validate and rank those data collaboratively. Scientilla promotes the concept that only working together is possible to build up a unique, correct and complete picture of Researchers and Organizations scientific productivity.

What we would like to offer:

  • Reference manager designed not only for a single unit but also for groups of people.
  • Manage audio, video and supplementary materials submitted by authors.
  • Ranking system through tags (data mining).
  • Datasets of publications, which you can easily export and import.

With a single tool Scientilla provides assistance in many research issues. Research information is fragmented across various systems and spreadsheets within an institution. This leads to administrative workload, inconsistent and incomplete data and unreliable reports, all of which adversely affect business decisions. With your help Scientilla ensures the data that drives your strategic decisions is reliable, comprehensive and accessible in real time. A highly versatile system, Scientilla will enable your organization to build reports, carry out performance assessments, manage researcher profiles, enable expertise identification and more, all while reducing the administrative burden for researchers, faculty and staff. Scientilla provides an extensive range of functions that any user can directly expand. Scientilla indeed is open source.

Scientilla’s Workflow:

  • Personal profile: create researchers or organizations or other profiles.
  • List of publications: capture data across the world of research.
  • Validation: certify your research information as validated and reliable.
  • Scientific profile: achieve precise, up to date, complete and reliable publication list with minimal effort.

Scientilla’s Next Steps:

  • ANALYSIS: track progress towards research milestones for groups and individuals and identify subject experts.
  • ADVISING: identify the journals in which it is better to publish according to several criteria.

It will be possible to export and import in each kind of format (RIS, BibTeX, csv.,.). It will be also possible to import from other programs like Zotero, EndNote…Each kind of analysis will be plotted with the most advanced graphics tools. Scientilla will allow you to configure, visualize and export information according to your personal needs and preferences, so that you can benchmark with meaning and accuracy to better understand your position with respect to your peers, as well as global and domestic standards.

Scientilla brings together data and technology and soon metrics and visualizations will follow. Designing and building such a solution requires extensive knowledge of data structuring and handling, significant investments in technology, a strong understanding of research management, excellent partnerships in research institutions and mainly your collaboration.

Why is it important your collaboration?

Only you know what your scientific production is. Scientilla team is aware that you are tired to view wrong data, affiliations and subject categories associated with your name and your research. You don’t have to start an endless correspondence of emails to ask for changes to your list of publications. With Scientilla you are responsible for your scientific profile, which will be visible all around the world.

Forever Free!


For Developers

Scientilla is a freely downloadable software that enables organizations and researchers to share through internet their bibliographic metadata and let the most reliable version of each publication description emerge thanks to a ranking algorhythm that orders the entire set of references by comparing them and counting similar/identical ones.

In other words the system allows every user to download in their machine the bibliographic metadata shared by others and automatically hide the noise relative to reference copies that are affected by typing mistakes and other errors. It is able to do that because each publication description is often, if not always, reported by more than one researcher or organization with its own version and probably the most correct and complete is the one that has been written more times in the exact same way.

Scientilla has been developed as a P2P (Peer to Peer) tool, which means each user can directly connect to someone else's installation to freely view, and eventually copy, their list of public bibliographic metadata. The architecture is not based on a central web server but relies instead on the multitude of installations where each one is identified by an URL and is then clearly reachable and responsible for what is shared.

In other words the network is made up of all software copies installed all around the world by organizations and researchers. The Scientilla-running computers form a grid of equally privileged nodes, each one publicly exposing the bibliographic information configured from the relevant owner, that in normal cases should fit with their scientific production.

Scientilla allows an installation owner to configure the list of their shared references using different modalities. One consists of manual insertion through a fillable form. Another concerns the search, copy and eventually correct references (cloning) from other nodes. The last concerns the import of publications from files in different formats or to integrate them from web services that are available on user's local networks or on the internet.

In each installation the following rules are then applied:

When a new reference is added to the list of an installation, eithr manually or importing/integrating the data, this is memorized together with an hash string calculated on the complete text of the publication description and called original_hash. When instead an already existing reference is copied from another node, that is stored with the original_hash of the source plus another hash string recalculated after any corrections and called clone_hash. Every time a reference is saved or updated it is also supplied with an additional hash string calculated on the profile data of the user that requested its creation. This extra information is called user_hash. By disallowing the existence of more than one publication with the same user_hash and original_hash the system is able to ensure that for each installation there can be only one reference for the same organization or researcher. When a shared reference is collected from another installation it is saved together with the source URL from which data are coming.
